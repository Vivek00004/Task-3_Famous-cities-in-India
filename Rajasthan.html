Rajasthan


Here are some details about Rajasthan, a state in northwestern India:
Size
Rajasthan is India's largest state by area, covering 342,239 square kilometers.
Location
Rajasthan is bordered by Pakistan to the west and northwest, and by the Indian states of Punjab, Haryana, Uttar Pradesh, Madhya Pradesh, and Gujarat to the east, southeast, and other sides.
Capital
The capital of Rajasthan is Jaipur, which is located in the east-central part of the state.
History
Rajasthan was previously known as Rajputana, which means "The Country of the Rajputs". It became the state of Rajasthan in 1949, and took its current form in 1956.
Economy
Rajasthan is primarily agricultural and pastoral, and is one of India's largest producers of wool. It also has a growing manufacturing and service sector.
Rajasthan is known for its rich culture, which includes many festivals and fairs. Some of the most popular festivals include Pushkar and Nagaur, which are world-famous cattle fairs.
Tourism
Rajasthan is a popular tourist destination, with many attractions including:
The Hawa Mahal, Jal Mahal, and Amer Fort in Jaipur
The cities of Jaisalmer and Jodhpur
Village tours
