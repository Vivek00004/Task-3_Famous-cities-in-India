Hyderabad(City of Pearls)

Here are some details about Hyderabad, the capital of Telangana state in India:
Location
Hyderabad is in the Deccan Plateau, in the northern part of South India, on the banks of the Musi River.
Climate
Hyderabad has a warm to hot, monsoonal climate with moderate annual precipitation. Most rain falls during the wet monsoon months of June to October.
Population
Hyderabad is the largest and most populous city in Telangana. The 2011 census of India reported a population of 6.9 million in the city limits and 9.7 million in the metropolitan region.
Economy
Hyderabad has the sixth-largest urban economy in India. Major industries include software, pharmaceuticals, electrical fans, cooling systems, and automotive industries.
Transportation
Hyderabad has good transportation facilities, including air and rail services to major cities in India. The city also has a light rail transportation system called the MultiModal Transport System (MMTS).
Attractions
Hyderabad has many attractions, including the Char Minar, Golconda Fort, Qutbshahi Tomb, Mecca Masjid, Osmania University, and HITEC City.
Culture
Hyderabad is known for its rich cultural and historical traditions. The city is associated with Telugu language movies production, and is sometimes called "Tollywood".
History
Hyderabad was founded in 1591 by Mohammed Quli Qutub Shah, the fifth sultan of Qutb Shahi dynasty. It was the capital of Andhra Pradesh from 1956 to 2014, and became the capital of both Telangana and Andhra Pradesh when Telangana was created in 2014.
