Delhi

Here are some details about Delhi, India:
Location
Delhi is a union territory in North India that straddles the Yamuna River, a tributary of the Ganges. It's bordered by the state of Uttar Pradesh to the east and the state of Haryana to the north, west, and south. Size
The National Capital Territory (NCT) of Delhi covers 1,484 square kilometers (573 sq mi).
Population
The city proper of Delhi had a population of over 11 million in 2011, while the NCT's population was about 16.8 million.
Transportation
Delhi is well connected by roads, rail, and air. It has three airports, three railway stations, and three inter-state bus terminals.
Climate
Delhi's climate is characterized by harsh summers, with temperatures reaching over 45Â°C from April to June. The monsoon season lasts until October.
History
Delhi has been known by many names, including Delhi, Dehli, Dilli, and Dhilli. The name likely comes from Raja Dhilu, a king who ruled in the region in the 1st century BCE.
Culture
Delhi is known for its art forms, including paper painting, miniature painting, manuscripts, and marble paintings. Textile embroidery is also a famous artwork from Delhi.
Government
New Delhi is the capital of India and the seat of all three branches of the Government of India.
