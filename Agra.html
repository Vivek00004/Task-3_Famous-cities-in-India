Agra

Here are some details about Agra, India:
Location
Agra is a city in the northern state of Uttar Pradesh, on the banks of the Yamuna River. It's 378 kilometers west of Lucknow, the state capital, and 206 kilometers south of New Delhi, the national capital.
History
Agra is steeped in history, with mentions in the Mahabharata and by the second century A.D. geographer Ptolemy. The city was founded by Sikandar Lodi in the 16th century, and was the capital of the Mughal empire for most of its history.
Monuments
Agra is known for its many monuments, including the Taj Mahal, Agra Fort, and Fatehpur Sikri, all of which are UNESCO World Heritage Sites. The city is also known for its tombs and mausoleums, and for its lavish crafts like Pietra Dura, rugs, and leather goods.
Tourism
Agra is a major tourist destination, and is part of the Golden Triangle tourist circuit, along with Delhi and Jaipur. The best time to visit Agra is September through December.
Fort
The Agra Fort is an architectural marvel, with a drawbridge, slight ascent, and 90-degree turn between the outer and inner gates. The fort is still used by the Indian military, and tourists enter via the Amar Singh Gate.
